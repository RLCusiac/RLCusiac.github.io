Hi, I’m Rebekka

PhD student/Amateur musician/Devoted climber

I am currently pursuing a PhD degree in psychology at the University of Western Ontario in music cognition and numerical cognition. My research mainly focuses on how people perceive and represent non-symbolic proportions/ratios in space and time.

In addition to my interest in how the brain works, I am also passionate about producing high quality psychology research. This includes learning how to create well-designed studies as well as becoming proficient in coding and statistics.

Me in a nutshell

Research

I am currently conducting research in the Music and Neuroscience lab led by Jessica Grahn, as well as the Numerical Cognition lab led by Daniel Ansari. Although my research is mainly on ratio processing, I am also involved other collaborative research projects studying music learning and the perception of large numbers.

Teaching

Since 2020, I have participated in the development of widgets for the educational website NEWBI4fMRI. Additionally, I have supervised multiple honor students conducting research projects in music cognition.

Coding workshops

Since 2021, I, along with a fellow graduate student, Stacey Reyes, started organizing social coding workshops in which graduate students more experienced in coding could share their knowledge with other graduate students new to coding.

Measurement and Statistics

I'm interested in measurement and statistics. Although my own research has used more complex analyses structural equation modeling (SEM), I also interested in effectively teaching basic statistics, notably through the use of interactive widgets.
