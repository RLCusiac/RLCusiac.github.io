How to evaluate an article

The title is a lie. I will not talk about how to critically evaluate the content or the validity of an article. Instead, I will focus on the characteristics that make up a solid, quality article I feel excited to have read.

Clear statement of the problem

Often articles assume that the reader are familiar with the field, and therefore mention or allude to some of the fundamental questions without clearly stating the link between the study and this overarching question.

Clear question and hypothesis

Clear logic between the analysis and the hypothsis

Example articles:

  • Binzak, John V., and Edward M. Hubbard. “No Calculation Necessary: Accessing Magnitude through Decimals and Fractions.” Cognition 199 (June 1, 2020): 104219. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cognition.2020.104219.
