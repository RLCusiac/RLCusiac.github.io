Common mistakes

Trying to read EVERYTHING

Your time limited, so unless you are conducting a systematic review of the literature, your goal is not to read every single article ever written in your field. You will have to make choices about what you decide to spend time reading and taking notes on.

Steps to conducting a literature review on an unfamiliar topic

1. Acquire breadth

The first thing you will want to do when diving into a new literature is to acquire an initial breadth of knowledge. There are a lot of unknown unknowns at this stage, therefore your goal is to:

  • Become familiar with key words and concepts: Often multiple different terms are used to refer to the same thing both within and across fields.
  • Develop an intuitive knowledge of underlying themes on the topic of interest.

2. Identify your articles of interest

Once you've read enough abstracts, Get good at skimming articles

3. Going deeper

Once you have found your golden nuggets, you can starting looking deeper into each study. For me, this includes looking at three things: the abstract, the intro (especially the current study section), and the methods.
