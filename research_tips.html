I have a study idea, now what...

From Idea to Hypothesis: Developping a research idea into a testable hypothesis

Fleshing out a preliminary idea

  1. Determine overarching question and operationalized question+hypotheses (what is the mechanism you are proposing)
  2. Write guiding questions for lit review: Thinking about this might lead to potential extensions of your study. Try to narrow done the questions the rationale or story the study will be telling.
  3. Flesh out task manupilations/conditions + try to put on paper what you want to compare (analyses/contrasts) → related to hypotheses
  4. Last bonus step: try to identify what assumptions you are making across everything you have written (e.g., assumptions when operationalising variables, elaborating a mechanism, are your planned contrast really testing what you are interested in)
  5. Consider alternative explanations: could anything in your design constitute a confound that will prevent you from eliminating one explanation? Does your design consistute a critical test of your idea? (e.g., can you use your mechanism to explain data going in more then one direction)

Tools for designing a solid experiment

Table of result

Often used in registered reports. Contains numbered questions and hypotheses

Conceptual diagram of relations

Often used in SEM

Anticipated results

E.g., ANOVA, type of interaction

Ideas to beware of

Ideas in which you will determine whether a correlation is true once add for all

Could still be a good depending on the quality of your approach (e.g., changing task variants might not bring a lot of insight, but using a technique that allows you to address a major limitation, like using SEM instead of regression, might)
